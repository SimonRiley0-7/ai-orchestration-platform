# FinGuard Adversarial Workflow Prompts

Below is a curated list of enterprise-grade prompts you can copy and paste into the **FinGuard Studio** to dynamically generate extremely complex banking and risk-assessment workflows.

---

### 1. The Cross-Border Wire Transfer (AML/Fraud Focus)
Use this prompt to demonstrate strict regulatory compliance handling, forcing the AI to evaluate geopolitical and chronological risk factors.

> **Prompt:**  
> "Design a strict Anti-Money Laundering (AML) wire transfer workflow. It needs to accept the `Transfer Amount`, `Sender Country`, `Recipient Country`, and `Receiver Account Age (Days)`. The engine must strictly deny any transfer over $50,000 going to a high-risk jurisdiction if the receiving account is less than 30 days old."

---

### 2. Startup Venture Capital Vetting (Investment Focus)
Use this prompt to show how the adversarial AI can handle corporate finance mathematics and aggressive investment thesis debates.

> **Prompt:**  
> "Create a Venture Capital series funding evaluation workflow. The inputs should be `Startup Sector`, `Monthly Burn Rate ($)`, `Current MRR ($)`, and `Months of Runway`. The Arbitrator should prioritize startups with at least 18 months of runway and an MRR over $20,000, while the Challenger should aggressively attack any high burn rate compared to low incoming MRR."

---

### 3. Commercial Real Estate Mortgage (Risk Focus)
Use this to demonstrate physical and geographical risk assessment, highlighting the engine's ability to reason about loan-to-value ratios and volatility.

> **Prompt:**  
> "Build a Commercial Real Estate (CRE) origination workflow. Inputs must include `Property Zip Code`, `Requested Loan Amount`, `Property Appraisal Value`, and `Current Occupancy Rate (%)`. Ensure the Advocate fights for approval if the loan-to-value ratio is extremely safe (under 50%), but the Challenger must mandate an automatic rejection if the occupancy rate is below 70% in a volatile zip code."

---

### 4. High-Net-Worth Wealth Account Opening (KYC Focus)
Use this to highlight Know Your Customer (KYC) onboarding logic and source-of-wealth validation.

> "Generate a High-Net-Worth private banking onboarding workflow. Required inputs: `Applicant Net Worth`, `Primary Source of Wealth`, `Politically Exposed Person (Boolean)`, and `Requested Account Type`. Deny onboarding if the applicant is a Politically Exposed Person (PEP) unless their net worth exceeds $10M and their source of wealth is 'Inheritance' or 'Verified Corporate Acquisition'."

---

### 5. Automated Overdraft Forgiveness Eligibility
Use this to demonstrate internal customer health checks and relationship banking logic based purely on their ledger history.

> **Prompt:**  
> "Design an Overdraft Fee Forgiveness workflow. The inputs are `Account Age (Years)`, `Number of Overdrafts in Past Year`, `Average Daily Balance ($)`, and `Current Direct Deposit (Boolean)`. The Advocate must fight to waive the fee if they have Active Direct Deposit and an Average Daily Balance over $1,500. The Challenger must aggressively deny forgiveness to anyone with more than 3 overdrafts in the past year, regardless of balance."

---

### 6. Credit Card Limit Increase Assessment
Use this to show how the AI handles continuous risk exposure based on internal credit utilization data.

> **Prompt:**  
> "Create a Credit Limit Increase workflow. The inputs required are `Current Limit ($)`, `Requested Limit ($)`, `Average Utilization Ratio (%)`, and `Late Payments (Past 24 Months)`. The engine must reject requests over a 50% jump from the current limit if the utilization ratio is already exceeding 80%. Escalate for human review if they have 1 late payment, but outright reject if they have 2 or more."

---

### 7. Mortgage Escrow Account Adjustment
Use this to demonstrate backend operational logic, analyzing highly specific internal banking variables.

> **Prompt:**  
> "Build a Mortgage Escrow Shortage adjustment workflow. Inputs: `Current Escrow Balance ($)`, `Projected Tax Increase (%)`, `Homeowner Policy Premium Jump ($)`, and `Customer Repayment Capability Indicator (1-10)`. The Arbitrator must mandate a lump sum payment if the projected tax increase exceeds 15% and the customer capability indicator is below 4. The Advocate should try to build an argument for spreading the shortage out over 12 months instead."
